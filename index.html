Andalucía

Andalucía located in the South-West of Spain is one of Spain's most amazing places. If you haven't been I would highly recommend getting in a car and driving from the famous cities through the small villages dotted throughout. Going by train or bus is also a great option, I've done all! Start in Sevilla and drive through Los Pueblos Blancos of Arcos de la Frontera and Ronda, swing by El Caminito del Rey and head towards Granada where you must stay for a few days! If you have longer make your way to Córdoba before heading back to Sevilla! Imagine orange blossom, 30+ degrees, Alcazars, Cathedrals and tapas - lots of tapas, some free! And don't forget a few cervezas and sangrias of course! Read on for some more on this itinerary!

Granada

I just love Granada! It is set in the most magnificent surroundings of the Sierra Nevada and the famous Alhambra overlooking the city. The most important part though is the free tapas! Yes free - with a drink that is! Surround yourself with Moorish architecture, get lost in the old winding streets and enjoy the outside culture here where you will most likely find yourself stopping off frequently for a cortado or a caña! Granadinos or Granadinas of course, don't like to eat too early so don't be surprised to find places still close for Siesta and don't reopen until late!

Sevilla

The capital of Andalucía, Sevilla sits on the banks of the Rio Guadalquivir and has many attractions including the famous imposing Cathedral and the Giralda tower, the Plaza de España and not forgetting the Alcazar, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The Old Town of Sevilla contains the Barrio Santa Cruz and should not be missed. Surround yourself with orange trees and more amazing food as you stroll around!

Córdoba

A bit of a hidden gem, Córdoba lies a couple of hours from both Granada and Sevilla. Famous for it's Moorish architecture, in particular the Mesquita which now houses a Cathedral, yes it's inside, Córdoba also is famous for it's many patio courtyards and the Alcázar de Los Reyes Cristianos. As you would have come to expect, many great tapas restaurants to be found and also the Mercado Victoria is worth a visit.
