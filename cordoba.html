Mesquita-Catedral

The Mesquita-Catedral or Mosque Cathedral is exactly that - a mosque with a cathedral in the middle! There is a ticketed entry however the best way to see it is at 8.30 in the morning when it is free and not too many people around! They do kick you out before 9.30 though so be quick! The bell tower is worth a visit too and is a couple of euros.

Alcázar

The Alcázar de los Reyes Cristiano is a Moorish palace or castle. Sitting next to the Rio Guadalquivir and containing walled gardens, the Alcázar can be visited via ticket only.

El Caminito del Rey

El Caminito del Rey is a fabulous walkway through gorges which will take a few hours. The hike is one way so park at El Chorro train station and get the bus to the starting point so that when you finish your car is there waiting for you! Get tickets in advance in order to be sure of a timeslot although it is possible to get in without.

Ronda

A couple of hours drive from Sevilla, Ronda is an impressive town sitting on the edge of a canyon. A good place to stay the night and drive around the many white villages nearby. Make sure to visit Arcos de la Frontera for lunch on the way.
